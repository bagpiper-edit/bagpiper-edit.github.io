Abstract

Current text-guided audio editing methods rely on paired training data, predefined operation templates, and separate processing pipelines across speech, music, and sound. We present Bagpiper-Edit to enable open-ended audio editing via free-form natural language instructions. We reformulate audio editing as a rich-caption rewriting task by treating a rich caption as the semantic representation of an audio clip. The user request is translated into an edited caption, which then guides Bagpiper-Edit to generate the target edited audio with the original audio as contextual acoustic anchor. This unlocks the potential of free-form editing, and circumvents the need for paired audio-editing training data, enabling powerful zero-shot editing capabilities. Evaluations across speech, audio, and free-form editing show Bagpiper-Edit maintains good consistency to the original audio and achieves similar performance to other expert models in most cases.

3. Training Pipeline 📄 Full Config (train.yaml)

Bagpiper follows a three-stage training pipeline. All stages use DeepSpeed ZeRO-1 with Adam optimizer, bfloat16 mixed precision, and activation checkpointing.

StageObjectiveFrozen Params LRBatch SizeGrad AccumScheduler
Stage 1 — Warmup Train multimodal adapters only Codec modules + all LM layers + text embeddings 1e-415 000 tokens1 Cosine (5k warmup → 600k, min 0.3)
Stage 2 — Pre-train Full-parameter pre-training Codec modules only 1e-415 000 tokens1 Cosine (5k warmup → 600k, min 0.3)
Stage 3 — SFT Task-specific fine-tuning Codec modules only 1e-512 000 tokens4 Cosine (350k warmup → 500k, min 0.2)

Key differences in Stage 3: Switches to Qwen3-8B-Base backbone with flash_attention_3. Learning rate is 10× lower (1e-5 vs 1e-4) with a very long warmup ramp from the pre-trained checkpoint. Gradient accumulation increases to 4 for effective larger batch size. The single-task (ST) variant trains to ~359k steps; multi-task (MT) to ~356k steps.

Classifier-free guidance (CFG): During training, 5% of audio conditions are randomly dropped (audio_cfg: 0.05) to enable CFG at inference time.

Decoding modes:

  • Audio generation — Standard auto-regressive decoding with CFG scale 3.
  • Audio continuation — Retains a prefix (80%) of the source audio and generates the remainder. Used for speech-level edits (transcription, style).
  • Speech (no CFG) — For pure speech tasks where CFG may over-constrain prosody.

5.3 Evaluation Dimensions

Speech editing tasks are judged on:

  • Consistency (1-5) — Preservation of speech content & speaker identity
  • Change Quality (1-5) — Execution quality of the requested change
  • Overall Score (1-5) — Holistic quality (not a simple average)

Non-speech audio tasks additionally use:

  • Edit Fidelity (1-5) — Was the edit correctly applied?
  • Audio Quality (1-5) — Production quality, artifacts, distortion
  • Coherence (1-5) — Scene coherence, naturalness of blend
  • Preservation (1-5) — Are non-edited elements intact?
